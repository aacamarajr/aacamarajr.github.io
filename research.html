I am currently a visiting Post-Doctoral Researcher at the Ecology Systematic Evolution Laboratory ESE at IDEEV and Universide Paris-Saclay, in Orsay, France. My research, in collaboration with Jeanne Ropars (CR CNRS) and supervised by Tatiana Giraud (DRCE CNRS), is focused on the identification of genomic-adaptive-evolutionary footprints in yeasts isolated from Brazilian artisanal cheeses, selected under stress conditions, and using a comparative genomics approach.

This period as a visitor at the ESE Laboratory is part of my Postdoctoral project started at the Quantitative Food Microbiology Laboratory LMQA at UNICAMP, in Campinas, Brazil. This project, supervised by Prof. Anderson Sant'ana (UNICAMP/FEA), aims mainly at the selection, identification and production of dehydrated yeasts from Brazilian artisanal cheeses.

My PhD in Food Biotechnology was defended in 2018 at Université Bourgogne Franche-Comté, France, and was supervised by Florence Husson (AgroSup Dijon) and Raphaëlle Tourdot-Maréchal (AgroSup Dijon). The main objective of my study was to understand the biophysical and biochemical resistance phenomena of oenological yeasts during dehydration, with a special attention to the biosynthesis of the protective substances glutathione and trehalose. The purpose of this study was to promote the production of dehydrated and active yeasts without using food additives.
